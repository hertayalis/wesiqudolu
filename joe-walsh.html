    Joe Walsh is a singer, guitarist, and composer from the United States. He has been a member of five successful rock bands in his 50-year career: James Gang, Barnstorm, Eagles, Party Boys, and Ringo Starr & His All-Starr Band. Walsh was also a member of the Herbs band from New Zealand. He was a part of the short-lived supergroup The Best in the 1990s.     Where is Joe Walsh born?

    Joseph Fidler Walsh was born in Wichita, Kansas, according to his biography. Walsh was adopted by his stepfather at the age of five after his biological father died in an aircraft crash. His mother was a professionally trained pianist of Scottish and German origin.

    During Walsh’s childhood, he and his family lived in Columbus, Ohio. Walsh’s family relocated to New York City when he was twelve years old. Walsh later relocated to Montclair, New Jersey.

    He received his schooling at Montclair High School, where he was a member of the school band and played the oboe. Following high school, Walsh attended Kent State University, where he played in different Cleveland-area bands, including the Measles. He then dropped out of university to pursue a career in music.
