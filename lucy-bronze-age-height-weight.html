    Lucy Bronze Age, Height, Weight: Lucy Bronze, officially known as Lucia Roberta Tough Bronze is an English professional footballer.

    She developed a passion for playing football at a younger age, has been consistent throughout her career, and has become one of the most sought-after professional footballers.

    At this time of writing (Monday, August 7, 2023), Bronze is a professional footballer who plays as a right-back for Liga F club Barcelona and the England women’s national team.

    Since turning pro, she has had stints with Sunderland, Everton, Liverpool, Lyon, and Manchester City as well as North Carolina at college level in the United States and Great Britain at the Olympics.

    Bronze played for the Sunderland youth team from 2002-2007 and their senior side from 2007-2010. She joined the Sunderland senior team when she turned 16 in 2007.

    She had a short stint with North Carolina Tar Heels (College) in 2009. Bronze played for Everton from 2010-2012 and went ahead to play for Liverpool from 2013-2014.

    In 2015, Bronze had her first stint with Manchester City. She signed for the team in November 2014. In 2016, her second season, Bronze she scored two league goals as City went unbeaten for the entire season.

    From 2017-18, Bronze played for Lyon. In August 2017, Bronze signed a three-year contract with Lyon. In the 2017–18 season of the UEFA Women’s Champions League, she made 8 appearances, scoring 2 goals

    During the 2018–19 season, Bronze made 29 appearances for Lyon in all competitions, scoring two goals

    At the end of the 2019 season, she finished as runner-up for the Women’s Ballon d’Or, was named UEFA Women’s Player of the Year Award, and finished third in the voting for The Best FIFA Women’s Player Award.

    Following the conclusion of her contract with Lyon and her trophy-laden three-year spell there, Bronze rejoined Manchester City in 2020 on a two-year deal expected to expire in 2022.

    In June 2022, Bronze agreed to join Barcelona after her contract with Manchester City expired. She scored her first goal for Barcelona in an Otro Clasico match against Atlético Madrid on 27 November 2022.

    On 30 April, Barcelona won the league at home with four matches to play and an all-win record. As of Monday, August 7, 2023, Bronze plays as a right-back for Liga F club Barcelona.

    She also plays as a right-back for the England women’s national team. On 31 May 2023, she was named to the squad for the 2023 FIFA Women’s World Cup in July 2023, her third consecutive World Cup appearance.

    Bronze has won four UEFA Women’s Champions League titles in total. She won the UEFA Women’s Champions League three times with Lyon and once with Barcelona.

    She also won three FA Women’s Super League titles with Liverpool and Manchester City. With England, Bronze won the UEFA Women’s Euro 2022.

    In 2019, she became the first English footballer to win the UEFA Women’s Player of the Year Award and won the inaugural Globe Soccer Award for the Women’s Best Player
